SiteVar and MitVarProt Algorithms

Site-specific variability indexes are calculated through the application of SiteVar (for nucleotide sequences)1 and MitVarProt (for aminoacid sequences)2, according to the following algorithm:

 
 

in which υi is the variability value estimated for each ith site of a dataset containing N sequences.

When applying this algorithm to nucleotide sequences, δ is a parameter whose value is 1 for transitions and 2 for both transversions and gaps in the ith position of the pair of sequences j, and Kj is the mean genetic distance calculated for the pair of sequences j on the entire alignment, according to the Kimura model.

When applying this algorithm to aminoacid sequences, δ is a mtREV index (expressing the level of similarity between two aminoacids) for the ith position of the pair of sequences j, and Kj is the mean genetic distance calculated according to the Kimura model.

In both cases, υi values are normalized with respect to the maximum value of variability (υmax) calculated in the considered dataset, thus obtaining a new value, γi, which makes variability indexes comparable among different datasets:
